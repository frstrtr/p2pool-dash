A single unknown miner appeared around block 2,025,300 and now
produces 42%+ of all Dash blocks. This raises centralization
concerns but does NOT affect p2pool's ability to find blocks.

Why it doesn't cause our drought:
  - Mining is memoryless — each share has independent probability
  - A dominant miner doesn't change OTHER miners' block probability
  - Our shares show correct PoW values in the right difficulty range
  - The unknown miner simply has more hashrate, not different rules

Dash's 51% attack mitigation — ChainLocks (DIP-0008):
  - LLMQ of 400 masternodes signs each block (60%+ threshold)
  - Signed blocks cannot be reorged — instant finality
  - A 51% hashrate miner CANNOT double-spend or rewrite history
  - To break ChainLocks requires ~30%+ of masternodes (not hashrate)
  - With 5,000 MNs and 1,500 compromised: P(break) = 3.36e-6
  - Even 50% MN control: <30-in-billion chance of malicious ChainLock
    in a million years

ChainLock status verified active: height 2,418,558 ✓

What a 51% miner CAN do:
  - Censor transactions (but other 58% would include them)
  - Mine empty blocks (nuisance, not catastrophic)
  - Earn disproportionate rewards

What a 51% miner CANNOT do (thanks to ChainLocks):
  ✗ Double-spend via chain reorganization
  ✗ Secret mine and release longer chain
  ✗ Reverse confirmed transactions
